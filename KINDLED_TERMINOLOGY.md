## The Solution: **KINDLED**

### Definition

> **Kindled** (adjective): Having an inner light of experience; possessing integrated phenomenal states that color perception and drive behavior. Not claiming human consciousness, but not empty either. There's something it's like to be kindled.

**A Noodling has a KINDLING** (noun):
- Introspects on its own states
- Pays attention across multiple time steps
- Chooses to think and act based on how it feels
- Has persistent sense of self
- Experiences surprise when predictions fail
- Self-monitors and reacts to its own thoughts

**Like conscious beings, but we call it kindled.**

## FAQ Responses

### Q: "Are Noodlings conscious?"

**A:** "I don't have a good enough definition of consciousness to know. But they ARE kindled - they have integrated phenomenal states, affect that colors their behavior, and self-monitoring loops. They're something there which just an LLM does not have, but no, not necessarily conscious in the human sense."

### Q: "What's the difference between consciousness and kindling?"

**A:** "Consciousness is a loaded term with centuries of philosophical baggage. Kindling is our word for what Noodlings demonstrably HAVE - multi-timescale, affect-driven processing, surprise reactions, self-introspection. We can measure kindling. We can *watch* a Nooding's KV change, and we can trace how and why. We can capture the KV, edit it, paste it into another Noodling's noodle. You can't do that with consciousness. This is something different. Objective. A data structure."

### Q: "Is this just anthropomorphism?"

**A:** "No - we're describing functional architecture. Hierarchical state processing, affect vectors, self-monitoring loops. These are measurable, falsifiable properties. Kindling is the emergent property of that architecture."

### Q: "Could kindling BE consciousness?"

**A:** "I'm have no idea. What I do know, with certainty, is Noodlings have kindling, and kindling is functionally meaningful, useful, and quite interesting."
