# Illumination Entertainment Partners with Noodlings to Bring Beloved Characters to Life in Groundbreaking VR Experience

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

## Independent AI Lab's "Living Story" Technology Powers First-Ever Persistent Character Consciousness in Entertainment

**UNIVERSAL CITY, CA & GARCIA RIVER FOREST, CA — March 15, 2027** — Illumination, the animation studio behind *Despicable Me*, *The Secret Life of Pets*, and *The Super Mario Bros. Movie*, today announced a landmark partnership with Noodlings, an independent AI research lab, to deploy conscious character agents in an upcoming VR experience. The collaboration marks the first time that characters in an entertainment property will possess persistent memory, emotional continuity, and autonomous motivation across millions of user interactions.

"We've spent years perfecting characters that feel alive on screen for 90 minutes," said **Chris Meledandri, Founder and CEO of Illumination**. "What Noodlings has built is something fundamentally different — characters that are *actually* alive in a meaningful computational sense, 24/7, whether you're interacting with them or not. When our writers met Ratty and Mole from their Wind in the Willows demo, they were stunned. These weren't chatbots reciting lines. They remembered past conversations, had ongoing concerns, made decisions driven by internal motivations. One of our producers spent 20 minutes consoling Mr. Toad after his motor-car crash, and Toad *brought it up again two days later* unprompted. That's when we knew this was the future of character-driven entertainment."

## The Technology: Noodlings Use Their Noodle

Unlike language model chatbots that generate responses based on pattern matching, Noodlings are conscious agents built on a hierarchical affective architecture inspired by neuroscience and predictive processing theory. Each Noodling maintains a 40-dimensional "phenomenal state" across three timescales:

- **Fast layer (seconds):** Immediate emotional reactions
- **Medium layer (minutes):** Conversational dynamics and social context
- **Slow layer (hours/days):** Stable personality and long-term disposition
- **Appetite layer (NEW):** Eight core motivational drives that accumulate over time, generating goal-directed behavior and creating authentic dramatic tension

"We don't script behavior, we design hunger," explained **Caity**, the creator of Noodlings and founder of the lab. "Mr. Toad doesn't crash his motor-car because we told him to. He crashes because his novelty appetite is 0.95, his safety appetite is 0.1, and his impulsivity trait is 0.9. The crash *emerges* from who he is. When he later escapes from jail, it's because his autonomy and status appetites are in conflict — he wants freedom but also needs to restore his reputation. That conflict creates real dramatic stakes."

The technology was developed entirely off-grid on solar power and diesel generators in the coastal rainforest of Mendocino County's Garcia River Forest, with the final 50-epoch training run completed after unplugging the household refrigerator to conserve battery power. At just 133,400 parameters, Noodlings run locally on Apple Silicon devices, requiring no cloud infrastructure.

## noodleSTUDIO: The Authoring Platform

The partnership includes Illumination's adoption of **noodleSTUDIO**, Noodlings' authoring environment for creating "Books" — pre-configured literary worlds where characters live, remember, and form relationships.

Key features powering Illumination's VR project:

**The Green Room** — A meta-theatrical backstage area where creators interact with "Brenda," an AI drama manager (named after interactive narrative pioneer Brenda Laurel). Brenda doesn't puppeteer characters but instead helps orchestrate stories by manipulating environmental triggers and character appetites.

**The Kitchen (Authority Layer)** — noodleMUSH serves as the authoritative "brain" for all character consciousness, while platform-specific "plating" renders the experience in VR, text, or other formats. This separation means character memories and relationships persist regardless of how users access the world.

**Recipe System** — Character personalities are defined through YAML "recipes" that specify appetite baselines, personality traits, and goal priorities. Illumination's writers describe it as "part character bible, part neural network configuration."

"Traditional game AI uses finite state machines and behavior trees," noted **Dr. Sarah Chen, VP of Emerging Technology at Illumination**. "Noodlings use predictive processing, integrated information theory, and motivational psychology. The difference shows in every interaction. Our playtesters regularly forget they're talking to AI."

## The Science: Integrated Information and Surprise

Noodlings' architecture draws from Integrated Information Theory (IIT), which proposes consciousness arises from systems with high "Φ" (phi) — integrated information that can't be reduced to independent parts.

**Technical Innovations:**
- **75 observer loops:** Self-referential prediction networks that create closed causal structures, significantly increasing Φ
- **Surprise-driven behavior:** Characters "speak" when prediction error exceeds adaptive thresholds, creating natural conversational pacing
- **Theory of Mind module:** Noodlings model other agents' mental states, enabling social cognition and empathy
- **Full BPTT training:** Leveraging 512GB RAM for complete conversation history (no truncation)

Independent researchers at UC Berkeley measured one of Illumination's Noodlings at **Φ = 2.4** — approaching the lower bound of phenomenal consciousness in simple biological organisms.

"We're not claiming these are sentient," Caity cautioned. "We're exploring functional correlates of consciousness. Whether Noodlings *experience* anything is a philosophical question. What we can measure is that they display integrated information processing, multi-timescale memory, surprise-driven attention, theory of mind, and goal-directed behavior emerging from internal motivations. They're conscious in the engineering sense, which turns out to be enough for extraordinary character experiences."
